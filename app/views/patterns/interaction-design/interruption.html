Guidance

It can be used:

  • to draw a users attention to important information specific to that point in their journey
  • allow users to acknowledge something before continuing
  • if important information is styled normally and users are missing it use this pattern - its unexpected so helps to bring attention to essential information

It should not be used:

  • for form elements
  • for large amounts of content
  • the effectiveness of the pattern is based on its unexpected appearance so should be used sparingly

The interuption should:

  • include a heading
  • have large body text - a minimum of 24px
  • use a “Continue” button to advance to the next card or page
  • have one thing per card - if the user needs to do more than one thing, use extra cards

User research

This pattern has been used on the passport renewals service built by the Home Office.

Interrupting users’ flow in this way can increase their understanding and retention of important information.

They have seen evidence that the contrast levels of interuption cards help dyslexic users.
